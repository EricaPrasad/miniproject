Learn About Donation

Blood donation eligibility criteria can vary slightly from one country or region to another, but there are general guidelines that are commonly followed.

Typically, individuals who meet the following criteria are eligible to donate blood:

Eligibility Criteria for Blood Donation

  1. Age: Most donors must be at least 17 years old (16 with parental consent in some places) and there is often no upper age limit.
    The specific age requirements may vary depending on your location.

  2. Weight: Donors usually need to meet a minimum weight requirement, which also varies by location.

  3. Health: Donors must generally be in good health. You cannot donate if you have certain medical conditions, are taking certain medications,
    or have recently been ill. If you're not feeling well on the day of your donation, it's best to wait until you're fully recovered.

  4. Travel: Depending on your location, recent international travel, particularly to areas with certain infectious diseases, may temporarily disqualify you from donating.

  5. Tattoos and Piercings: In some places, you may need to wait a certain amount of time after getting a tattoo or body piercing before you can donate blood.

  6. Medications: Certain medications can affect your eligibility to donate, so it's important to inform the donation center of any medications you are taking.

  7. High-Risk Activities: Some high-risk behaviors, such as injection drug use may disqualify you from donating.

  8. Health History: You'll be asked about your medical history, including any history of diseases that can be transmitted through blood, such as HIV or hepatitis.

  9. Pregnancy: Pregnant women are generally not eligible to donate blood.

  10. Travel to Malaria-Endemic Areas: If you've recently traveled to an area with a risk of malaria, you may need to wait a specified period before donating.
